Vision

I develop computational models and machine learning methods to understand how neural circuits in the brain give rise to perception, learning, and decision-making. My work spans building foundation models for brain activity, designing novel neural integral equation architectures for spatiotemporal data, and uncovering how cortical neuromodulatory signals change during learning.

Research

Key Research Areas

My research combines deep learning, mathematical modeling, and neuroscience to tackle fundamental questions about brain computation.

01

Foundation Models for Brain Activity

BrainLM is a foundation model trained on 6,700 hours of fMRI recordings. It predicts clinical variables, forecasts future brain states, and discovers functional networks through zero-shot inference.

ICLR 2024
02

Neural Integral Equations

Attentional Neural Integral Equations (ANIE) learn unknown integral operators from data, providing a principled framework for modeling spatiotemporal dynamics in physical and biological systems.

Nature Machine Intelligence 2024
03

Cortical Cholinergic Signaling & Learning

Spatiotemporal modeling of cortical cholinergic (ACh) and calcium signaling dynamics reveals selective changes in neuromodulatory patterns that emerge during associative learning.

bioRxiv 2025
04

Robustness & Generalization in Neural Networks

Robust deep learning models rely on low-frequency information in natural images. Local convolutions induce an implicit bias toward high-frequency adversarial examples via the Fourier Uncertainty Principle.

PLOS Comp. Bio. 2023 · Frontiers 2024
